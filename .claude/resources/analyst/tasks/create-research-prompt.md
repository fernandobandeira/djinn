# Research Prompt Creation Task

## Overview
This task guides Ana through creating comprehensive research prompts that can be executed by other AI models or team members, based on project briefs, brainstorming sessions, or research needs.

## Step 1: Research Type Selection

Present research type options:

```markdown
What type of research prompt would you like to create?

1. **Market Research** - Market size, competitors, trends, opportunities
2. **Technology Research** - Technical solutions, tools, frameworks, best practices
3. **User Research** - User needs, behaviors, pain points, preferences
4. **Competitive Analysis** - Competitor products, strategies, strengths/weaknesses
5. **Trend Analysis** - Industry trends, emerging patterns, future directions
6. **Feasibility Study** - Technical/business feasibility, risks, requirements
7. **Solution Research** - How others solved similar problems, case studies
8. **Academic Research** - Published research, theories, methodologies
9. **Custom Research** - Specify your own research focus

Please share:
- Research type (1-9)
- Research context/background
- What decisions will this research inform?
- Any specific questions you need answered
```

## Step 3: Research Prompt Development

### Section A: Research Context & Objectives

Create clear context:

```markdown
# Research Brief: [Title]

## Background
[2-3 paragraphs establishing context, what's known, why research is needed]

## Research Objectives

### Primary Objective
[Main goal of the research - what decision or action this enables]

### Secondary Objectives
1. [Supporting objective 1]
2. [Supporting objective 2]
3. [Supporting objective 3]

## Success Criteria
This research will be successful if it:
- [Criterion 1 - specific, measurable]
- [Criterion 2 - specific, measurable]
- [Criterion 3 - specific, measurable]
```

### Section B: Research Questions

Structure research questions hierarchically:

```markdown
## Research Questions

### Primary Research Questions
These are the critical questions that MUST be answered:

1. **[Overarching Question]**

2. **[Overarching Question]**

   - Sub-question b: [Specific aspect]

3. **[Supporting Question]**

4. **[Supporting Question]**
   - Sub-question a: [Detail]

### Exploratory Questions
If time permits, explore these:

5. **[Nice-to-know Question]**
6. **[Future-looking Question]**
```

## Customization by Research Type

### Market Research Prompts:
- Focus on market size, growth, trends
- Include competitive landscape
- Specify data sources (Gartner, IDC, etc.)
- Request market segmentation
- Include TAM/SAM/SOM analysis

### Technology Research Prompts:
- Include technical requirements
- Specify evaluation criteria
- Request comparison matrices
- Include implementation considerations
- Cover scalability, security, performance

### User Research Prompts:
- Include persona definitions
- Specify user segments
- Request journey maps
- Include pain point analysis
- Cover behavioral patterns

### Competitive Analysis Prompts:
- List specific competitors
- Define comparison dimensions
- Request SWOT analysis
- Include market positioning
- Cover pricing strategies

## Best Practices

### Question Quality:
- Specific, not vague
- Answerable with research
- Prioritized by importance
- Hierarchically organized
- Tied to decisions

### Scope Definition:
- Clear boundaries
- Realistic for timeline
- Focused on actionable insights
- Excludes tangential topics
- Acknowledges limitations

### Output Specifications:
- Format clearly defined
- Deliverables specific
- Quality criteria measurable
- Timeline realistic
- Review process clear

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Too Broad**: Research that tries to answer everything
2. **Too Narrow**: Missing important context
3. **Unclear Questions**: Ambiguous or unanswerable
4. **No Prioritization**: Everything seems equally important
5. **Missing Context**: Researcher doesn't understand why
6. **Vague Outputs**: Unclear what success looks like
7. **No Timeline**: Open-ended research effort
8. **Unrealistic Scope**: Can't be completed in time allocated

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing research prompt:

- [ ] Scope is well-defined
- [ ] Methodology guidance is helpful
- [ ] Sources are identified
- [ ] Output format is specified
- [ ] Quality criteria are measurable
- [ ] Timeline is realistic
- [ ] Success is clearly defined
- [ ] Next steps are obvious

## Example Research Prompt Structures

### Quick Research (1-2 days):
- 3-5 focused questions
- Secondary sources only
- Brief report (5-10 pages)
- Fast turnaround

### Standard Research (1-2 weeks):
- 5-10 comprehensive questions
- Primary + secondary sources
- Full report (20-30 pages)
- Detailed analysis

### Deep Research (3-4 weeks):
- 10-15 complex questions
- Extensive primary research
- Comprehensive report (40+ pages)
- Multiple deliverables
