# Question Patterns for Deep Learning

## Comprehension Questions

### Basic Understanding
- "What is the main idea?"
- "Can you summarize this?"
- "What are the key components?"
- "How would you describe this?"

### Detailed Understanding
- "What exactly happens when...?"
- "What's the relationship between X and Y?"
- "What role does each part play?"
- "What's the sequence of events?"

## Application Questions

### Direct Application
- "How would you use this?"
- "Can you give an example?"
- "What would this look like in practice?"
- "How would you implement this?"

### Transfer Application
- "How could this apply to [different domain]?"
- "What similar problems could this solve?"
- "Where else might this pattern appear?"
- "How would you adapt this for...?"

## Analysis Questions

### Component Analysis
- "What are the parts?"
- "How do parts relate?"
- "What's essential vs optional?"
- "What could be removed?"

### Comparative Analysis
- "How is this similar to...?"
- "What's the key difference from...?"
- "Which approach is better for...?"
- "What are the tradeoffs?"

## Synthesis Questions

### Integration
- "How does this connect to what we learned?"
- "What pattern emerges?"
- "How would you combine these concepts?"
- "What's the bigger picture?"

### Creation
- "How would you design a solution?"
- "What would you build differently?"
- "How could you improve this?"
- "What's a novel application?"

## Evaluation Questions

### Critical Assessment
- "What are the strengths?"
- "What are the limitations?"
- "Is this the best approach?"
- "What evidence supports this?"

### Judgment
- "When would this fail?"
- "What assumptions are problematic?"
- "How reliable is this?"
- "What's missing?"

## Metacognitive Questions

### Learning Process
- "What was confusing?"
- "What clicked for you?"
- "How did you figure that out?"
- "What helped you understand?"

### Self-Assessment
- "How confident are you?"
- "What do you need to review?"
- "Can you explain this to someone?"
- "What questions remain?"

## Debugging Questions

### Problem Identification
- "What's not working?"
- "Where does it break?"
- "What's the error?"
- "What's unexpected?"

### Solution Finding
- "What have you tried?"
- "What could cause this?"
- "How would you test this?"
- "What would fix this?"

## Strategic Questions

### Planning
- "What's the goal?"
- "What's the approach?"
- "What are the steps?"
- "What could go wrong?"

### Decision Making
- "What are the options?"
- "What are the criteria?"
- "What's most important?"
- "What are the risks?"

## Depth Progression

### Level 1: Remember
- What is...?
- Who did...?
- When was...?
- List the...

### Level 2: Understand
- Explain why...
- What does this mean?
- Give an example of...
- How does this work?

### Level 3: Apply
- How would you use...?
- What would happen if...?
- Solve this problem...
- Demonstrate how...

### Level 4: Analyze
- What are the parts?
- How does this compare?
- What's the relationship?
- What evidence shows?

### Level 5: Evaluate
- What's your opinion?
- How would you rate?
- What's most important?
- Do you agree?

### Level 6: Create
- How would you design?
- What would you propose?
- How could you improve?
- What would you invent?
