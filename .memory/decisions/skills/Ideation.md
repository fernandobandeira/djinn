# Ideation

**Tier:** Universal

## Core Principle

**Quantity enables quality.** Generate many options before judging any—the best idea often comes from the worst one.

## Problem

Brainstorming is unfocused and unproductive:
- Same ideas get recycled
- Groups converge too quickly
- Creative thinking is constrained by assumptions
- No structure leads to shallow exploration

## Solution

Structured creativity techniques that break conventional thinking patterns.

**Techniques:**
- **SCAMPER** - Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Reverse
- **Walt Disney Method** - Dreamer, Realist, Critic phases
- **Reverse Brainstorming** - How to cause the problem, then flip solutions

## Supported By

- [[Role Playing]] - Walt Disney uses Dreamer/Realist/Critic roles; stepping into different perspectives unlocks creative thinking

- [[Devils Advocate]] - Embedded throughout ideation techniques:
  - **SCAMPER**: Eliminate and Reverse are challenge moves ("what if we remove this?", "what if we flip this?")
  - **Walt Disney**: Critic phase IS devils advocate
  - **Reverse Brainstorming**: The entire technique is devils advocate ("how do we make this worse?")

## Why It Matters

- **Universal need** - Any agent generating ideas benefits
- **Breaks patterns** - Techniques force new perspectives
- **Structured exploration** - Methodical coverage of idea space
- **Quantity before quality** - Diverge before converging
