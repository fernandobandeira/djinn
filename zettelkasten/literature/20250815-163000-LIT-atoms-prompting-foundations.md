## Key Points
### Main Argument
Atomic prompts are the fundamental building blocks of LLM interaction, consisting of single instructions that follow the pattern: TASK + CONSTRAINTS + OUTPUT FORMAT. While limited, they establish the baseline for measuring context engineering improvements.

### Supporting Evidence
1. **Power Law Relationship**: Token-quality curve shows maximum ROI zone followed by diminishing returns
2. **Inherent Limitations**: No memory, limited demonstration capability, prone to ambiguity, high variance
3. **Implicit Context**: Models leverage massive training knowledge even in atomic prompts
4. **Measurement Framework**: Atomic prompts provide control baseline for context engineering experiments

### Important Quotes
> "Models struggle with consistency when given minimal context."
> — Context: Validates need for more sophisticated approaches

> "There's a power law relationship between context tokens and output quality"
> — Context: Foundational principle for context optimization strategies

> "Moving beyond atoms is the first step toward context engineering"
> — Context: Sets up progression to molecular approaches
