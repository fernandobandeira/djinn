# Engineering Challenge Equals Ambiguity Resolution

## Core Insight
The fundamental engineering challenge in context design is not about adding more information to prompts, but about systematically reducing uncertainty and ambiguity. This reframes context engineering as a precision discipline focused on eliminating interpretive confusion rather than maximizing informational content.

## Evidence & Examples
### Concrete Example
Traditional approach: "Here's my code, here's my database schema, here's my requirements, here's my constraints..." (adds information but creates decision paralysis)
New approach: "I need to optimize this specific Django ORM query that's causing 2-second page loads" (eliminates ambiguity about domain, technology, and success criteria)

### Abstract Pattern
Engineering problems are solved by reducing variables and constraints, not by adding more complexity to consider.

### Edge Cases
Some problems genuinely require broad context, but these are fewer than commonly assumed.
